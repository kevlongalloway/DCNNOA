01 — Mission

President's
Message

Our people are the asymmetrical advantage our services bring in all domains.

Welcome to the Washington DC Chapter (DCNNOA) of the National Naval Officers Association. For the past 250 years, the strength of our sea services has been central to our national security.

NNOA has been a key stakeholder in recruiting, developing, and retaining the best, most capable officer corps in the Navy, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard — committed to talent development from pre-accessioning scholarships through mid-career counseling and on to support for senior leaders.

DCNNOA is a premiere NNOA chapter that relentlessly promotes talent development through our annual Scholarship Program and our annual VA Hospital Visit with donations of essential clothing items to veterans.

November 11, 2025

Annual Veterans Day
Hospital Visit

On Veterans Day 2025, DCNNOA and VFW Post 9619 conducted their Annual Veterans Day visit to the Washington DC Veterans Hospital. Eighteen chapter members spent the morning meeting, greeting, and thanking veterans receiving medical care — providing approximately $7,000 worth of winter clothing and essential items.

05 — Timeline

Service
Timeline

1972
NNOA Founded

The National Naval Officers Association is established to support minority naval officers and promote diversity in the Sea Services.

2003
Scholarship Launched

DCNNOA launches its annual Scholarship Program for DMV high school seniors, beginning a legacy of investing in future leaders.

2010
VA Hospital Tradition

The annual Veterans Day visit to the Washington DC Veterans Hospital becomes a hallmark DCNNOA community event.

2020
Virtual Operations

Chapter meetings transition to virtual via Zoom, ensuring continuity of mission during unprecedented times.

2025
$1.15M Milestone

DCNNOA surpasses $1.15 million in total scholarships awarded to 671 students. VA Hospital donations reach $7,000 annually.

2026
The Next Watch

The 2026 Scholarship Program opens, continuing the mission of developing the next generation of Sea Service leaders.
